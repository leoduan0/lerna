# Cross Product

<Rule
  statement={
    <MathExpression
      math="\\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \det{\begin{bmatrix} \vec{i} & \vec{j} & \vec{k} \\ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \end{bmatrix}} = \lVert \vec{a} \rVert \lVert \vec{b} \rVert \sin(\theta)"
      type="block"
    />
  }
  terms={[
    {
      term: '\\vec{a}',
      annotation: (
        <span>
          A vector. Equals{' '}
          <MathExpression math="\langle a_1, a_2, a_3 \rangle" type="inline" />.
        </span>
      ),
    },
    {
      term: '\\vec{b}',
      annotation: (
        <span>
          A vector. Equals{' '}
          <MathExpression math="\langle b_1, b_2, b_3 \rangle" type="inline" />.
        </span>
      ),
    },
    {
      term: '\\theta',
      annotation: (
        <span>
          Angle between <MathExpression math="\vec{a}" type="inline" /> and{' '}
          <MathExpression math="\vec{b}" type="inline" />
        </span>
      ),
    },
  ]}
  annotation={
    <ul>
      <p>
        If <MathExpression math="\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \vec{u}" type="inline" />
        , then <MathExpression math="\vec{u}" type="inline" /> is a vector that is
        orthogonal to both <MathExpression math="\vec{a}" type="inline" /> and{' '}
        <MathExpression math="\vec{b}" type="inline" />.
      </p>
      <p>
        If <MathExpression math="\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = 0" type="inline" />
        , then <MathExpression math="\vec{a}" type="inline" /> and{' '}
        <MathExpression math="\vec{b}" type="inline" /> are parallel to each other.
      </p>
      <p>
        Cross products only exist in <MathExpression math="\R^3" type="inline" /> and{' '}
        <MathExpression math="\R^7" type="inline" />.
      </p>
    </ul>
  }
/>

<Block text="Example">

If $$ \vec{a} = \langle 1, 2, 3 \rangle $$ and $$ \vec{b} = \langle 4, 5, 6 \rangle $$, then $$ \vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \det{\begin{bmatrix} \vec{i} & \vec{j} & \vec{k} \\ 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix}} = (12 \vec{i} + 12 \vec{j} + 5 \vec{k}) - (15 \vec{i} + 6 \vec{j} + 8 \vec{k}) = -3 \vec{i} + 6 \vec{j} - 3 \vec{k} = \langle -3, 6, -3 \rangle $$.

</Block>
