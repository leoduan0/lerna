# Vector

A **vector** can be used to represent a magnitude and a direction.

## Notation

A vector with $$ n $$ components is denoted by $$ \vec{a} = \langle a_1, a_2, a_3, ... , a_n \rangle $$.

<Block text={'Example'}>

A 2-dimensional vector $$ \vec{a} $$ with x-component of 2 and y-component of 4 is denoted by $$ \vec{a} = \langle 2, 4 \rangle $$

</Block>

<Block text={'Example'}>

A 3-dimensional vector $$ \vec{b} $$ with x-component of 2, y-component of 4, and z-component of 6 is denoted by $$ \vec{b} = \langle 2, 4, 6 \rangle $$

</Block>

<Block text={'Example'}>

A 4-dimensional vector $$ \vec{c} $$ with x-component of 2, y-component of 4, z-component of 6, and a-component of 8 is denoted by $$ \vec{c} = \langle 2, 4, 6, 8 \rangle $$

</Block>

<Block text={'Example'}>

A 1-dimensional vector $$ \vec{d} $$ with x-component of 2 is denoted by $$ \vec{d} = \langle 2 \rangle $$

</Block>

1-dimensional vectors are not scalars

## Vectors Between Points

A vector from point $$ A = (x_A, y_A) $$ to point $$ B = (x_B, y_B) $$ is $$ \vec{AB} = \langle x_B-x_A, y_B-y_A \rangle $$

Note that $$ \left( \vec{AB} = \langle x_B-x_A, y_B-y_A \rangle \right) \nRightarrow \left(\vec{BA} = \langle x_A-x_B, y_A-y_B \rangle \right) $$.

<Block text={'Example'}>

If $$ A = (4, 3) $$ and $$ B = (17, 14) $$, then $$ \vec{AB} = \langle 17-4, 14-3 \rangle = \langle 13, 11 \rangle $$ and $$ \vec{BA} = \langle 4-17, 3-14 \rangle = \langle -13, -11 \rangle $$.

</Block>

## Unit Vectors

For any vector $$ \vec{a} $$, if $$ \lVert \vec{a} \rVert = 1 $$, then $$ \vec{a} $$ is a **unit vector**.

<Block text={'Example'}>

If $$ \vec{a} = \langle 0, 0, 1 \rangle $$, then $$ \vec{a} $$ is a unit vector since $$ \lVert \vec{a} \rVert = \sqrt{0^2+0^2+1^2} = 1 $$.

</Block>

<Block text={'Example'}>

If $$ \vec{a} = \langle \sqrt{1}, 0, 2, 0 \rangle $$, then $$ \vec{a} $$ is not a unit vector since $$ \lVert \vec{a} \rVert = \sqrt{\sqrt{1}^2+0^2+2^2+0^2} = \sqrt{5} $$.

</Block>

<Rule
  statement={

    />
  }
/>

## Zero Vectors

For any vector $$ \vec{a} $$, if all the components of $$ \vec{a} $$ is 0, then $$ \vec{a} $$ is a zero vector.
