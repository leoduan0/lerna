# Multiple Integral

**Multiple Integrals** are similar to integrals in $$ \R^2 $$ except that the integration takes place in more than just two dimensions.

For a function $$ f(x, y) $$, its integration of some area $$ S $$ is denoted as:

<Align type={'center'}>
  <Math math={'\\iint\\limits_S f(x, y) \\,dA'} type={'block'} />
</Align>

Suppose that $$ S=[a,b] \\times [c,d] $$, then:

<Align type={'center'}>
  <Math
    math={
      '\\iint\\limits_S f(x, y) \\,dA = \\int_a^b \\int_c^d f(x, y) \\,dy\\,dx = \\int_c^d \\int_a^b f(x, y) \\,dx\\,dy'
    }
    type={'block'}
  />
</Align>

If $$ f(x, y)=g(x)h(y) $$, then:

<Align type={'center'}>
  <Math
    math={
      '\\iint\\limits_S f(x, y) \\,dA = \\left(\\int_a^b g(x)\\right)\\left(\\int_c^d h(y) \\,dy\\right)'
    }
    type={'block'}
  />
</Align>
