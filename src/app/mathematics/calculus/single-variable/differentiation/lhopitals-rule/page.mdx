# L'H么pital's Rule

**L'H么pital's rule** states that:

<Align type={'center'}>
  <Math
    math={
      '\\lim_{x \\to c} \\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \\lim_{x \\to c} \\frac{f^{\\prime}(x)}{g^{\\prime}(x)}'
    }
    type={'block'}
  />
</Align>

L'H么pital's rule only works if direct substitution results in any of the following **indeterminate forms** and would not necessarily yield the correct result if otherwise:

- $$ \frac{0}{0} $$
- $$ \frac{\infty}{\infty} $$
- $$ 0 \times \infty $$
- $$ \infty - \infty $$
- $$ 0^0 $$
- $$ 1^\infty $$
- $$ \infty^0 $$
