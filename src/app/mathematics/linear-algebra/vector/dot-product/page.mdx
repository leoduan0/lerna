# Dot Product

<Rule
  statement={

      math={

  terms={[

<Block text={'Example'}>

If $$ \vec{a} = \langle 3, 6, 9 \rangle $$ and $$ \vec{b} = 8 \vec{i} - 5 \vec{k} $$, then $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = \langle 3, 6, 9 \rangle \cdot \langle 8, 0, -5 \rangle = 3 \cdot 8 + 6 \cdot 0 + 9 \cdot -5 = -21 $$.

</Block>

<Block text={'Example'}>

If $$ \vec{a} = 15 \vec{i} + 3 \vec{j} + 4 \vec{k} $$ and $$ \vec{b} = \langle 0, 0, 0 \rangle $$, then $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = \langle 15, 3, 4 \rangle \cdot \langle 0, 0, 0 \rangle = 15 \cdot 0 + 3 \cdot 0 + 4 \cdot 0 = 0 $$.

</Block>

<Block text={'Example'}>

If $$ \lVert \vec{a} \rVert = 5 $$ and $$ \lVert \vec{b} \rVert = 36 $$, and the angle between them is $$ 60\degree $$, then $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 5 \cdot 36 \cdot \cos(60\degree) = 90 $$.

</Block>

<Block text={'Example'}>

If $$ \lVert \vec{a} \rVert = 100 $$ and $$ \lVert \vec{b} \rVert = 100 $$, and the angle between them is $$ 90\degree $$, then $$ \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 100 \cdot 100 \cdot \cos(90\degree) = 0 $$. Note that $$ \vec{a} $$ and $$ \vec{b} $$ are orthogonal to each other.

</Block>
