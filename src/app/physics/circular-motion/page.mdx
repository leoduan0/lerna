# Circular Motion

**Circular motion**, much like linear motion, is the study of the motion of rotating objects.

Linear properties can be compared to its circular counterpart and vice versa.

| Linear Property | Linear Symbol   | Circular Property            | Circular Symbol           |
| --------------- | --------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Mass            | $m$             | Inertia                      | $I=\int r^2 \, dm$        |
| Displacement    | $s$             | Angular displacement (angle) | $\theta$                  |
| Velocity        | $v=\frac{s}{t}$ | Angular Velocity             | $\omega=\frac{\theta}{t}$ |
| Acceleration    | $a=\frac{v}{t}$ | Angular Acceleration         | $\alpha=\frac{\omega}{t}$ |
| Force           | $F=ma$          | Torque                       | $\tau=I\alpha$            |

## Kinematics Equations

All four equations of kinematics can be used for circular motion as well. To use them, simply substitute the linear motion properties with its circular motion equivalents. _e.g._ $$ \omega_f^2 = \omega_i^2 + 2\alpha \theta $$ is the circular counterpart of $$ v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2as $$.
