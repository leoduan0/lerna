# Thermodynamics

## Laws of Thermodynamics

### Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics

<Rule
  statement={
    <p>
      If two systems are both in thermal equilibrium with a third system, then
      all three systems are in thermal equilibrium with each other.
    </p>
  }
/>

### First Law of Thermodynamics

<Rule
  statement={<Math math={'\\Delta U = Q + W'} type={'block'} />}
  terms={[
    {
      term: '\\Delta U',
      annotation: <span>Change in the internal energy of the system</span>,
    },
    {
      term: 'Q',
      annotation: <span>Heat transferred into the system</span>,
    },
    {
      term: 'W',
      annotation: <span>Work done on the system</span>,

  ]}
/>

### Second Law of Thermodynamics

<Rule
  statement={
    <p>
      Heat always, spontaneously and naturally, flow from hotter objects to
      colder ones.
    </p>
  }
/>
<Rule
  statement={
    <p>
      When two objects are placed in contact with one another, the total final
      entropy is more than or equal to the initial entropy in both objects added
      together.
    </p>
  }
/>

### Third Law of Thermodynamics

<Rule
  statement={
    <p>
      A system's entropy approaches a constant value as its temperature
      approaches <Math math={'0K'} type={'inline'} />.
    </p>
  }
/>

## Entropy

**Entropy** is the how evenly distributed energy within a given system is. A system with more evenly distributed energy has higher entropy than another with less.

## Ideal Gas

<Rule
  statement={
    <>
      <p>
        An <strong>ideal gas</strong> is a theoretical, idealized model of a gas
        that satisfies the following conditions:
      </p>
      <ul>
        <li>
          The particles of an ideal gas are assumed to be point-like, meaning
          they have no volume.
        </li>
        <li>
          Ideal gas particles do not interact with each other except during
          collisions.
        </li>
        <li>
          Particles in an ideal gas move randomly in straight lines until they
          collide with each other or with the walls of the container.
        </li>
        <li>Particles in an ideal gas obey Newton's laws of motion.</li>
        <li>Collisions between ideal gas particles are perfectly elastic.</li>
      </ul>
    </>
  }
/>

### Thermodynamic Processes

| Process    | Description          | Equation             |
| ---------- | -------------------- | -------------------- |
| Isothermal | Constant temperature | $$ \Delta U = 0 $$   |
| Isobaric   | Constant pressure    | $$ W = P \Delta V $$ |
| Isochoric  | Constant volume      | $$ W = 0 $$          |
| Adiabatic  | No heat transfer     | $$ Q = 0 $$          |
